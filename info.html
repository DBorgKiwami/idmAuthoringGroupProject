Your Healthcare Rights

Understanding your entitlements and healthcare coverage in Ireland

European Health Insurance Card (EHIC)

If you're from an EU/EEA country or Switzerland, your EHIC entitles you to medically necessary healthcare in Ireland on the same terms as Irish residents.

✓
Emergency treatment

Access to emergency departments and urgent care

✓
GP services

Same fees as Irish residents for GP visits

✓
Hospital care

Public hospital services at standard rates

Healthcare for Non-EU Residents

If you're from outside the EU/EEA, you may need to pay the full cost of healthcare unless you have private insurance or a bilateral agreement.

Important: Emergency care is always available regardless of ability to pay.

Typical Costs

Emergency Department €100+
GP Visit €50-€80
Hospital Inpatient €1,000+
Specialist Consultation €150-€300

Recommendations

1. Purchase travel or health insurance

2. Keep medical receipts

3. Check bilateral agreements

4. Register with a local GP if staying long term

Medical Cards & GP Visit Cards

Medical Card

Allows access to most health services free including GP visits and medications.

Eligibility
  • Income assessment
  • Automatic for certain groups
  • Resident in Ireland

GP Visit Card

Provides free GP visits but prescriptions must still be paid.

Eligibility
  • Higher income threshold
  • GP consultation only
  • Must live in Ireland

Your Rights as a Patient

Respect & Dignity

You must be treated with respect and dignity.

Information

You have the right to clear information about treatment and costs.

Consent

You can give or refuse consent for treatment.
